Community Outreach

Team 2898 is involved within our community, spreading STEAM in various ways, from robot demos at local elementary schools to organizing and running summer camps for the youth in our community to even building and developing a robot that chases geese out of an area! Here is a list of our Outreach Projects:

Spark Academy

Team 2898 and their sister team, Team 1510 The Wildcats, work together over the summer to run and teach summer camps for students in grades 4 through 8. These camps cover a variety of topics from Graphic Design to Computer Programming. For more information, here is a link to the Spark Website.

Robot Demos

Many companies and schools are interested in what we do as a Robotics team, and we’re always eager to show them what Team 2898 can do. Companies and schools we have demoed at include: ASML, Autodesk, etc etc

BSD Science Expo

The Beaverton School District hosted a Science Expo at Tektronix this past year, and Team 2898 was honored to have the chance to demo one of their robots there. Over 100 students and their families presented their science fair projects, and well as engage in the various STEM activities available for them to explore.

Adventure Camp Summer 2017: Mad Scientist Week

Over the summer of 2017, Team 2898 was invited to organize and present a day’s worth of STEM-activities to over eighty kids in grades K to 5. The Hedgehogs led several stations including a shoebox-car making activity, as well as a robot demo, and a 3D-Printing demo. This was the most popular week for the camp.

Goosebot

Team 2898 is partnering with Tektronix, a local tech company, to build and develop the Goosebot, a fully autonomous robot whose function is to chase geese off of their property.
