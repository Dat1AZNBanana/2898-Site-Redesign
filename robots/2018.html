FRC 2018 | FIRST Power Up

The Game

FIRST Power Up was the FIRST Competition Game for the 2018 season. In Power Up - a retro, arcade themed game - two alliances of three robots would compete by placing milk crates, or “power cubes,” on large balancing scales. Teams scored points for tipping these scales to gain ownership. Alliances could also trade power cubes for “power-ups” to gain temporary advantages during matches. At the end of each match, robots would have to opportunity to climb the tower at the center of the scale to gain extra points.

Our Robot - Out of Order

Our Power Up robot went through several major changes throughout the 2018 competition season. For our first design iteration and competition, the robot had a combination lift, with a continuous first stage and cascading second stage. This elevator design was tailored to fit our original strategy - to focus on placing cubes in the scale, but still have the versatility and capability to load the exchange and the switch.

We altered this feature before our second district competition, turning our bot into an exchange and switch-only robot. This new version was designed to be fast, light, and robust. Our final robot was equipped with a simple intake arm, a 6-wheel “west coast drive” style drivebase, and the ability to place one or more cubes in the switch during autonomous. It also boasted some impressive control and programming features, including LIDAR (LIght Detection And Ranging) sensors on the intake, reactive diagnostics, and a custom 3D-printed arm controller.
